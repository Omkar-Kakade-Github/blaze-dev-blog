Building a Plain Blog on Purpose

Personal websites tend to drift toward marketing. A little polish becomes a landing page, and a landing page starts demanding product language even when the site is really just a notebook with links.

I prefer a blog that behaves like a document: quick to load, easy to scan, and stable enough that old posts still feel readable a few years later. That means plain navigation, simple typography, and no decorative components competing with the writing.

The constraint is useful. If the page only offers text, structure, and a few links, the writing has to carry its own weight.
