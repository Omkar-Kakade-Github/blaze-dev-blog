Notes on Learning in Public

Learning in public does not require polished essays. It mostly requires a habit of stopping after a useful debugging session and writing down what changed in your head.

A practical template is enough:

  1. What problem was I trying to solve?
  2. What assumption was wrong?
  3. What would I check first next time?

If I can answer those three questions clearly, I usually have the beginning of a post.
